hydro3D

HIGH ORDER LARGE EDDY SIMULATION FOR ENVIRONMENTAL HYDRAULICS

Welcome to the hydro3D project.

hydro3D is a finite difference Navier Stokes solver that permits accurate and efficient simulation of turbulent flows. It has been developed by Prof Thorsten Stoesser and collaborators Cardiff University, UK, and Georgia Institute of Technology, USA, primarily for use in the simulation of civil and environmental fluid dynamics. Features

  • Sub-grid Scale (SGS) turbulent stress modelling using Smagorinsky or WALE methods.
  • Choice of spatial discretisation schemes including 4th order central differences.
  • Choice of temporal solution schemes including 5th order ENO/WENO.
  • Efficient computation of flows in and over complex geometries using the Immersed Boundary Method.
  • Efficient multigrid solver with MPI and OpenMP parallelisation.
  • Local mesh refinement for increased efficiency and accuracy.

Ongoing development

Bubble column simulation using Immersed Boundary particle tracking. 1-equation SGS modelling. Free surface simulation in turbulent flows using the Level Set Method.
